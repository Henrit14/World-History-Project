Decimal System Notation


One mathematical innovation created during the Gupta period was the development of the decimal system notation. Unlike previous systems, this notation enabled the representation of numbers using a base 10 system, laying the foundation for modern arithmetic. However, what is interesting about this period is that there was no use of the number zero, which is necessary for mathematical accuracy.


Numerals used in the Gupta Empire


To fix this issue, Aryabhata, a visionary mathematician recognized the necessity of zero in mathematical calculations and devised a clever workaround. Utilizing null coefficients within powers of ten, Aryabhata effectively introduced zero into numerical notation. This groundbreaking innovation not only facilitated more accurate computations but also revolutionized mathematical thinking, setting the stage for future advancements.
